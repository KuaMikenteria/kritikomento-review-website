Art Reviews

Oblation

By Napoleon Abueva

The Oblation (Filipino: Pahinungod, Oblasyon) is a concrete statue by Philippine National Artist Guillermo Tolentino that serves as one of the symbols of the University of the Philippines. It depicts a man facing upward with arms outstretched, symbolizing a selfless offering of oneself to his union.

Rating: 5/5

Filipino Struggles Through History

By Carlos “Botong” Francisco

Filipino Struggles Through History by Carlos “Botong” Francisco is a series of murals illustrating key events in Philippine history, from colonization to independence. Through vibrant and dynamic art, it celebrates Filipino resilience, heroism, and patriotism.

Rating: 4.8/5

Bonifacio Monument

By Guillermo Tolentino

The Andrés Bonifacio Monument, created by Guillermo Tolentino, is a 45-foot-tall sculpture in Caloocan, Philippines, honoring revolutionary leader Andrés Bonifacio. It features Bonifacio wearing a barong Tagalog, holding a bolo and a revolver, and standing amid ripe rice stalks. The monument also includes Emilio Jacinto, symbolizing the 1896 Philippine Revolution.

Rating: 4.9/5

Darna

By Mars Ravelo

Mars Ravelo's Darna is a 1950 Filipino comic about Narda, a girl who transforms into the superheroine Darna by swallowing a magical stone. Known for her strength and iconic red costume, Darna fights villains like Valentina and defends justice, becoming a cultural icon in the Philippines.

Rating: 4.8/5

People Power Monument

By Eduardo Castrillo

People Power Monument, created by Eduardo Castrillo, commemorates the 1986 People Power Revolution in the Philippines. Located at the EDSA Shrine in Quezon City, it features a dynamic sculpture of figures symbolizing unity, courage, and democracy, with a central female figure holding a cross, representing peace and freedom.

Rating: 5/5

Madonna of the Slums

By Vicente Manansala

Vicente Manansala's Madonna of the Slums is a 1950s painting that depicts a mother and child in a poor urban setting, reimagining the traditional Madonna and Child theme. Using the cubist style, Manansala portrays the struggles of post-war Filipino life, emphasizing resilience and maternal love amid poverty.

Rating: 4.8/5

Pugad Baboy

By Apolonio "Pol" Medina Jr.

Pugad Baboy by Apolonio "Pol" Medina Jr. is a popular Filipino comic strip that began in 1988. Known for its satire and humor, it depicts the lives of a community of overweight characters and tackles societal issues, politics, and Filipino culture with wit and relatable storytelling.

Rating: 4.5/5

Matrikula

By Gloc-9

"Matrikula" by Gloc-9 is a socially conscious rap song that highlights the struggles of Filipino families in affording education. It reflects on the sacrifices parents make to pay tuition fees and critiques the inequalities in the education system. Through powerful storytelling and emotional lyrics, the song resonates with listeners facing similar challenges, making it a significant piece in Gloc-9's discography.

Rating: 4.8/5

Spoliarium

By Juan Luna

Spoliarium by Juan Luna is a large painting depicting dead gladiators being dragged from a Roman arena, symbolizing pain, suffering, and defeat. It reflects Luna's socio-political views and patriotism against Spanish colonization, using contrasting colors like red and green to evoke emotion and harmony.

Rating: 5/5

Trese

By Budjette Tan

Trese by Budjette Tan is a Filipino graphic novel series blending urban fantasy, horror, and crime noir. It follows Alexandra Trese, a detective solving supernatural crimes in modern Manila, facing creatures from Filipino folklore. Celebrated for its storytelling and art, it highlights Philippine mythology and inspired a Netflix adaptation.

Rating: 4.8/5
